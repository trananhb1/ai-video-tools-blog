How We Calculated Cost Per Minute

Every tool below was priced on its cheapest paid plan with monthly billing (not annual) so the comparison is apples-to-apples for someone testing a tool for the first time. We note annual pricing separately where the savings are significant.

The formula is simple:

Cost per minute = Monthly subscription price ÷ Minutes of video included per month

For credit-based tools (Runway, Pika, Kling), we converted credits to approximate minutes using the tool's standard generation mode at default settings. For tools that measure output in "videos per month" (Pictory), we used the maximum video length to calculate total available minutes.

What counts as a "minute": One minute of finished, exportable, non-watermarked video. If the plan includes a watermark, we note it but still calculate the cost per minute of actual output.

All prices were verified from official pricing pages between April 25–28, 2026. Prices are in USD.

Assembler Tools: The Cheapest Per Minute ($0.08–$0.56/min)

Assembler tools do not generate original video frames. They combine stock footage, text overlays, AI voiceover, and music into finished videos. This is why they are dramatically cheaper per minute — the compute cost of stitching existing clips is a fraction of generating pixels from scratch.

Pictory — $0.08/min (Annual) | $0.10/min (Monthly)

Pictory is the cheapest AI video tool per finished minute in April 2026. The Starter plan at $19/month (annual) includes up to 30 videos per month, each up to 10 minutes — that is 240 potential minutes of output. Even if you only produce 10 full-length videos, you are still under $0.20/min.

Pictory excels at blog-to-video and script-to-video workflows. You paste a URL or script, Pictory pulls relevant stock footage, adds captions, and generates an AI voiceover. The output looks professional enough for social media and course content. It is not suitable for cinematic AI generation or avatar video.

Hidden cost to watch: The 14-day trial converts to a paid plan automatically if you do not cancel. Monthly billing jumps to $25/month ($29 for Professional).

Fliki — $0.12/min (Annual) | $0.16/min (Monthly)

Fliki costs slightly more than Pictory per minute but offers 2,000+ AI voices across 75+ languages — the best multilingual coverage of any assembler tool. The Standard plan at $21/month (annual) includes 180 minutes of video per month.

Fliki's strength is non-English content. If you need Hindi, Arabic, or Portuguese voiceover that sounds natural, Fliki beats every competitor in this price tier. The editor is simple but limited — do not expect Descript-level control.

Hidden cost to watch: AI avatars are only available on the Premium plan ($66/month annual). If you need an on-screen presenter, Fliki's cost per minute jumps significantly.

InVideo AI — $0.44/min (Annual) | $0.56/min (Monthly)

InVideo AI sits between assembler and template tools. The Plus plan ($28/month) includes 50 AI-generated minutes per month, plus 5,000+ templates and iStock footage. For social media managers producing multiple short videos per week, the per-minute cost drops further because template-based videos do not count against AI minutes.

Hidden cost to watch: The free tier adds a visible InVideo watermark. Unused AI minutes do not roll over.

Avatar Tools: Mid-Range Per Minute ($0.47–$2.90/min)

Avatar tools generate AI-powered digital presenters that lip-sync to scripts. They cost more per minute because the AI must render a realistic human face, sync lip movements, and maintain expression consistency across the entire video. The quality gap between cheap and premium avatar tools is enormous.

D-ID — $0.47/min (Lite Plan)

D-ID's Lite plan at $4.70/month is the cheapest avatar tool by sticker price, but it only includes 10 minutes per month and the avatar quality trails HeyGen and Synthesia noticeably. D-ID's strength is its API ($5.90/min), which developers use for chatbot integrations and kiosk applications. For video production, the photo-to-video feature is novel but uncanny.

Hidden cost to watch: The Lite plan is annual billing only. The Plus plan at ~$16/month is a more realistic option for regular use. Custom avatar creation is a paid add-on.

Vidnoz — $1.00/min (Starter) | $0.83/min (Annual)

Vidnoz offers 1,900+ avatars and a generous 3-minute/day free tier with no credit card required. The Starter plan at $14.99/month includes 15 minutes of avatar video. Avatar quality is noticeably below HeyGen's Avatar V on close-ups but holds up well for internal videos and social ads.

Hidden cost to watch: Free tier includes a watermark. Maximum video length is 5 minutes on Starter.

HeyGen — $1.93/min (Monthly) | $1.60/min (Annual)

HeyGen is not the cheapest avatar tool, but it offers the best avatar quality in the category. Avatar V (launched April 2026) has a 0.840 face similarity score — the highest benchmarked in any commercial AI avatar platform. The Creator plan at $29/month includes approximately 15 minutes of premium avatar video (200 credits, 13 credits/min for Avatar V).

For most buyers, HeyGen's quality-to-cost ratio makes it the best value in avatar video despite the higher per-minute cost. For our full pricing breakdown, see HeyGen Pricing 2026.

Hidden cost to watch: Credits do not roll over month-to-month. Premium features like Avatar V and translation consume credits faster than standard avatars. Credit packs cost $15 for 300 additional credits.

DeepBrain AI — $2.40/min (Personal Plan)

DeepBrain AI starts at $24/month (Personal) with unlimited video exports up to 30 minutes each and 60 generative credits per month. The most photorealistic AI avatars we have tested outside of HeyGen Avatar V, with particular strength in real-time avatar applications for kiosks and live broadcasts.

Hidden cost to watch: The generative credit system means AI-enhanced features (gesture control, multi-angle) consume credits faster. The Team plan at $69/month is a significant jump.

Colossyan — $2.70/min (Monthly) | $1.90/min (Annual)

Colossyan is an L&D-focused avatar platform with branching scenarios and SCORM export. The Starter plan at $27/month includes 10 minutes of video. The value proposition is not raw cost per minute but the training-specific features that HeyGen and Synthesia do not offer at this tier.

Hidden cost to watch: Custom studio avatars cost $1,000/year. The Business plan at $70–87/month is required for unlimited minutes.

Synthesia — $2.90/min (Monthly) | $1.80/min (Annual)

Synthesia is the enterprise standard for AI avatar video, with SOC 2 Type II compliance, SCORM export, and SAML SSO. The Starter plan at $29/month includes 10 minutes per month (120 min/year). Creator at $89/month includes 30 min/month, dropping the per-minute cost to $2.97 on monthly billing or ~$2.13 on annual.

Synthesia's per-minute cost is the highest among avatar tools, but for enterprise buyers the compliance features justify the premium. See our HeyGen vs Synthesia comparison for a detailed breakdown.

Hidden cost to watch: Express-1 custom avatar costs $1,000/year (annual plans only). Free plan includes a Synthesia watermark. Minutes are measured per year, not per month, on the Starter plan — so theoretically 120 minutes could be front-loaded.

Generative AI Tools: Most Expensive Per Minute ($2.54–$7.20/min)

Generative tools create entirely new video frames from text or image prompts using diffusion or transformer models. This is the most compute-intensive form of AI video, which is why it costs 10–90x more per minute than assembler tools. The output is also fundamentally different — cinematic, artistic, and creatively flexible in ways stock-footage assemblies cannot match.

Kling AI — $2.54/min (Standard) | $2.04/min (Annual)

Kling AI offers the best value in generative AI video. The Standard plan at $6.99/month includes 660 credits. In Standard mode, generating video costs approximately 240 credits per minute (4 credits/second), yielding roughly 2.75 minutes of output. Kling 3.0 (early access on Ultra plan) produces the best motion consistency we have seen outside of Veo 3.1.

The free tier is genuinely usable: 66 credits per day, resetting every 24 hours — enough for 2–3 short test clips daily.

Hidden cost to watch: Pro mode (higher quality) costs 7 credits/second, nearly doubling the per-minute cost. Kling 2.6 with native audio generation costs up to 5x more credits. Credits do not roll over.

Pika 2.2 — $5.26/min (Standard) | $4.21/min (Annual)

Pika's Standard plan at $10/month includes 700 credits. A typical 5-second, 1080p video using Pika 2.2 costs about 12 credits, meaning a full minute (twelve 5-second clips) costs roughly 144 credits. That yields approximately 4.86 clips or 1.94 minutes of footage per month. Pika excels at short creative clips — TikTok cutaways, B-roll shots, product hero shots — rather than long-form video.

Hidden cost to watch: Higher-quality modes (Pikascenes) cost 35–100 credits per generation. Free tier clips are watermarked. Maximum clip length is 10 seconds.

Runway Gen-4 — $7.20/min (Standard) | $5.76/min (Annual)

Runway is the most expensive AI video tool per minute in this comparison. The Standard plan at $15/month includes 625 credits. Gen-4 uses 5 credits per second (300 credits per minute), yielding roughly 2.08 minutes of output. Gen-4 Turbo uses fewer credits but at lower quality. The quality of Gen-4 output is among the best available — consistent characters, cinematic camera moves, and 1080p resolution up to 20 seconds.

For our full review, see Runway Review 2026.

Hidden cost to watch: Overages are $0.01 per credit ($3 per additional minute). Gen-4.5 (not yet in Standard plan) uses 12 credits per second, making it 2.4x more expensive per minute. The 125-credit free tier is one-time, not monthly.

Best Value Picks by Use Case (April 2026)

Best for Budget Creators: Pictory ($0.08/min annual)

If you need volume — repurposing blog posts, producing course content, or filling a social calendar — Pictory's 240 minutes per month at $19/month (annual) is unmatched. You sacrifice creative control and avatar quality for pure output per dollar.

Best for Solo Creators: HeyGen ($1.60/min annual)

If you need to appear on camera without a camera, HeyGen's Avatar V at $24/month (annual) delivers the most realistic AI avatar available. The 15-minute monthly allowance fits a weekly YouTube schedule or daily short-form clips. Pair with Submagic for captions for a complete faceless YouTube stack.

Best for Enterprise Training: Synthesia ($1.80/min annual)

Synthesia costs more per minute than HeyGen, but SOC 2 compliance, SCORM export, and SAML SSO mean your IT and procurement teams will actually approve it. For L&D teams producing 10+ training modules per quarter, the Enterprise plan removes minute limits entirely.

Best for Filmmakers and Creatives: Kling Standard ($2.04/min annual)

Kling delivers the best generative AI video quality-to-cost ratio. The free tier lets you experiment daily, and the Standard plan at $5.60/month (annual) is 3.6x cheaper per minute than Runway while producing comparable motion quality (though Runway still leads on character consistency).

Best for Multilingual Content: Fliki ($0.12/min annual)

Fliki's 75+ language voiceover at $21/month annual is ideal for global brands producing the same video in multiple languages. At 180 minutes per month, a 5-minute video in 12 languages uses 60 minutes — a third of your allowance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest AI video tool per minute in 2026?

Pictory is the cheapest at $0.08 per minute on annual billing ($19/month, 240 min/mo). Fliki is second at $0.12/min ($21/month annual, 180 min/mo). Both are assembler tools that combine stock footage with AI voiceover.

How much does AI-generated video cost per minute in 2026?

It ranges from $0.08/min (Pictory, assembler) to $7.20/min (Runway Gen-4, generative). Avatar tools fall in the middle at $0.47–$2.90/min. The 90x gap exists because assembler tools stitch stock footage while generative tools create original frames from scratch.

Which AI video tool offers the best value per minute?

It depends on your use case. For volume content: Pictory ($0.08/min). For avatar video: HeyGen ($1.60/min annual) has the best quality-to-cost ratio. For generative AI video: Kling ($2.04/min annual) beats Runway and Pika on value.

Are there hidden costs with AI video tools?

Yes. Common hidden costs include credit overages (Runway: $0.01/credit), watermarks on free tiers, resolution caps at 720p, custom avatar add-ons ($1,000/year on Synthesia and Colossyan), and storage limits on editors like Descript and VEED.

Is it cheaper to pay annually or monthly?

Annual billing saves 17–33% across most tools. HeyGen: $24/mo vs $29/mo (17%). Runway: $12/mo vs $15/mo (20%). VEED: ~$12/mo vs $18/mo (33%). Start monthly to test, switch to annual once confirmed.
