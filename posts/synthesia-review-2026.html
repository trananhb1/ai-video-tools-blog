What Is Synthesia?

Synthesia is an AI-powered video creation platform that lets you produce professional presenter-style videos using realistic AI avatars and text-to-speech, without ever stepping in front of a camera. You type a script, pick an avatar, choose a language, and Synthesia generates a polished video in minutes. No filming, no editing timeline, no production crew.

Founded in 2017 by a team of AI researchers from University College London and Technical University of Munich, Synthesia has grown into the most widely adopted AI avatar platform in the enterprise space. Over 50,000 companies use it, including names like Xerox, BBC, Nike, Heineken, and Amazon. In 2023, the company raised $90 million at a $1 billion+ valuation, cementing its position as the category leader.

The core value proposition is simple: traditional corporate video production is expensive, slow, and difficult to update. A single five-minute training video can cost $5,000 to $15,000 and take weeks to produce. Synthesia lets you create the same video in 15 minutes for a fraction of the cost, and when the content needs updating, you just edit the script and regenerate. For organizations producing training, compliance, onboarding, or multilingual content at volume, the math is compelling.

If you are producing professional, information-dense video content where consistency, scalability, and multilingual reach matter more than creative flair, Synthesia is built for exactly your workflow.

Key Features: What Makes Synthesia Stand Out

I have tested every major AI avatar platform on the market, and Synthesia consistently leads in avatar realism, language coverage, and enterprise readiness. Here are the features that matter most.

Synthesia offers the largest library of stock AI avatars in the industry. They span a wide range of ages, ethnicities, and professional styles. Custom Avatar Creation

On Creator and Enterprise plans, you can create a custom AI avatar of yourself or a team member. Record a short video following Synthesia's guidelines, and within a few business days you have a digital twin that can deliver any script in any supported language. This is a game-changer for companies that want a consistent on-screen presenter without scheduling filming sessions.

140+ Languages with Lip-Sync

This is Synthesia's most defensible competitive advantage. The platform supports over 140 languages and accents, and the lip-sync adapts to each language naturally. You write your script in English, click translate, and your avatar delivers the content in Japanese, Arabic, Portuguese, or Hindi with matched lip movements. No other platform comes close to this breadth.

AI Script Assistant

Synthesia includes a built-in AI script writing tool that helps you draft, refine, and translate video scripts. Paste your bullet points or a rough draft, and the assistant generates a camera-ready script with proper pacing and structure. It is not a replacement for a skilled writer, but it speeds up first drafts significantly and handles translations well.

Screen Recording Overlay

For software tutorials and product demos, Synthesia lets you overlay an AI avatar onto a screen recording. The avatar appears in a corner of the screen narrating the walkthrough, similar to a picture-in-picture presenter. This eliminates the need to record yourself separately and composite the footage in a video editor.

Enterprise and Creator plans include brand kit functionality. Upload your company logo, define brand colors and fonts, and every video you create automatically follows your visual identity. One-Click Translations

Take any existing Synthesia video and translate it into another language with a single click. The avatar re-renders with the new language, matching lip movements to the translated audio. This means a 10-video training library can become a 50-video multilingual library in an afternoon. The time savings over re-filming or dubbing are enormous.

Synthesia integrates with popular LMS platforms (Cornerstone, SAP, etc.), PowerPoint (import slides as video backgrounds), and offers an API for custom workflows. Collaboration features let multiple team members work on videos, leave comments, and manage approvals. It slots into existing enterprise toolchains without friction.

Who Should NOT Use Synthesia

Synthesia is excellent at what it does, but it is not the right tool for everyone. Be honest about whether your needs align with its strengths before committing to the subscription.

  • Solo creators on a budget. At $22-67/month with limited minutes, Synthesia is expensive for individual content creators. If you are a YouTuber, blogger, or freelancer, tools like HeyGen, Pictory, or Fliki offer more value at lower price points. Synthesia's premium is built for business budgets, not personal ones.
  • Creative and artistic video makers. If you want cinematic visuals, artistic transitions, dynamic motion graphics, or emotionally expressive storytelling, Synthesia is not the tool. Its output is professional and clean, but deliberately corporate in style. There is no way to produce content that feels organic, spontaneous, or visually adventurous.
  • Social media marketers wanting trending content. Synthesia videos look polished and corporate, which is perfect for LinkedIn and internal platforms but out of place on TikTok, Instagram Reels, or YouTube Shorts. The avatar format does not lend itself to the fast-paced, personality-driven content that performs on social media. Tools like CapCut, InVideo, or Zebracat are better fits.
  • Anyone needing text-to-video generation. Synthesia only creates avatar-based talking head videos. If you need AI to generate video clips from text descriptions (like Runway, Kling, or Sora), Synthesia cannot do it. It is a presenter tool, not a generative video tool.

Real Limitations You Should Know About

Most Synthesia reviews read like marketing copy. Here are the honest limitations I encountered during testing that you need to factor into your decision.

Avatar realism breaks down in close-ups. In medium and wide shots, Synthesia's avatars are impressively lifelike. But zoom in, and the cracks appear. Micro-expressions feel slightly mechanical, eye movements follow predictable patterns, and hand gestures are limited to a small set of canned animations. For training videos where the avatar occupies a small portion of the screen alongside slides, this is not an issue. For content where a face fills the frame, viewers will notice.

The creative ceiling is low. Synthesia gives you templates, backgrounds, text overlays, and screen recording integration. What it does not give you is creative freedom. You cannot create dynamic animations, custom transitions, split-screen layouts, or anything that deviates from the "presenter + slides" format. If your brand's video content needs to feel distinctive or creative, Synthesia will produce competent but generic output.

Rendering time adds up. Each video takes several minutes to render after you finalize the script. For a single video, this is fine. But if you are producing 20 videos in a session, the wait times accumulate. There is no batch rendering feature, so you submit one video, wait, then start the next. Enterprise plans get priority rendering, which helps but does not eliminate the bottleneck.

The free plan is nearly useless. Three minutes of video for the entire lifetime of your free account is barely enough to test the platform. Unlike Descript's generous free tier or D-ID's monthly renewal, Synthesia's free plan feels designed to frustrate you into upgrading rather than letting you genuinely evaluate the product. Budget at least one month of the Starter plan for a proper trial.
