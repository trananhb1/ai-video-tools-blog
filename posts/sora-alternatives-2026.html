Why is OpenAI shutting down Sora?

On March 24, 2026, OpenAI announced that Sora would shut down in two phases. The decision was driven by four factors:

  • Unsustainable compute costs. Video generation consumed GPU capacity that OpenAI needed for its core language model products.
  • Declining users. Active users dropped from 1 million at peak to under 500,000, according to TechCrunch reporting.
  • Copyright headwinds. Ongoing litigation over training data created legal risk that OpenAI chose not to carry.
  • Enterprise and IPO pivot. OpenAI is redirecting resources toward coding agents and enterprise products ahead of its planned IPO.

The practical impact: every creator, marketer, and developer who relied on Sora needs a replacement before April 26. How to migrate from Sora (step-by-step)

Step 1: Export your Sora data immediately

Log into your OpenAI account, go to Settings > Data Controls > Export, and download the ZIP file containing all your generated clips and the prompts that created them. You have until April 26, 2026. After that, stored outputs are permanently deleted. Save your prompts separately — they will help when testing new platforms. API users have until September 24, 2026.

Step 2: Match your use case to a tool

Sora users fall into distinct workflow buckets. Here is the fastest migration path for each:

  • Avatar/spokesperson videos: HeyGen (Avatar V is a direct upgrade over using Sora for talking-head content)
  • Corporate training: Synthesia (SOC 2, SCORM, governed workflows)
  • Cinematic text-to-video: Kling AI (cheapest) or Runway (most control)
  • Social media clips: Pika (fastest generation, social-optimized)
  • Blog-to-video / voiceover: Fliki (script in, video out, 75+ languages)
  • Marketing ad variants: Zebracat (bulk ad generation for paid social)

Step 3: Adapt your prompts

Sora prompts will not produce identical results on other platforms. Key differences:

  • Kling benefits from shorter, structured prompts. Specify character details (clothing, age, features) explicitly.
  • Runway uses a style reference system — pair text prompts with reference images for consistent results.
  • Pika works best with concise, action-focused prompts. Avoid overloading with detail.
  • HeyGen and Synthesia use scripts, not prompts — write what you want the avatar to say, not what you want the scene to look like.

Step 4: Test free tiers first

All seven tools on this list offer free tiers or trials. Take your five most representative Sora prompts and test them on two or three alternatives before committing to a paid plan. This costs nothing and will tell you more than any review can.

Pro tip: Many former Sora users find that a combination of two tools works better than any single replacement. For example, Kling for hero content + Pika for rapid social clips covers most workflows at under $15/month combined.
