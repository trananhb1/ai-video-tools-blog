Credits vs. Subscriptions: Two Very Different Pricing Models

The biggest source of confusion in AI video pricing is the split between credit-based and subscription-based tools. Understanding the difference is critical to avoiding bill shock.

Credit-Based Tools: Runway, Kling, and HeyGen

Runway uses a credit system where different operations consume different amounts. Generating a 5-second Gen-3 Alpha clip costs approximately 50 credits. On the Standard plan (625 credits/month), that works out to roughly 12 to 13 five-second clips per month, or just over a minute of total generated footage. On the Pro plan (2,250 credits), you get about 3.5 minutes. If you exceed your allocation, Runway charges $0.50 per additional credit — and those overages add up fast.

Kling operates similarly but is significantly more generous. Its free tier provides 66 credits daily (resetting each day), and its Pro plan at $25.90/month gives 3,000 monthly credits. A standard 5-second video generation costs around 10 credits on Kling, making it roughly five times cheaper per clip than Runway for comparable quality.

HeyGen uses credits tied to video minutes. One credit equals roughly one minute of avatar video. The Creator plan gives you 15 credits (about 15 minutes of video) for $29/month, while the Business plan provides 60 credits for $89/month.

Key takeaway: Credit systems reward careful usage but punish overages. If your video production volume is unpredictable, a flat subscription may be safer. If you can estimate your monthly needs precisely, credits can be more cost-effective, especially with Kling's generous allocations.

Subscription-Based Tools: Synthesia, Pictory, Descript, and Others

Synthesia gives you a fixed number of video minutes per month. The Starter plan includes 10 minutes for $29/month. Go over, and you pay approximately $3 per additional minute — making overages very expensive. The upside is predictability: you know exactly what 10 minutes of AI avatar video will cost.

Pictory counts videos rather than minutes. The Starter plan allows 30 videos per month at $23/month. Each video can be up to 10 minutes long, which is generous if you are creating shorter content. This makes Pictory one of the better values on paper, though the per-video limit can feel constraining if you are iterating frequently.

Descript bills based on transcription hours rather than video output, making it unique. The Hobbyist plan at $24/month includes 10 hours of transcription, which covers editing, captioning, and AI-powered features. For teams that primarily edit existing footage rather than generate new video from scratch, this model works well.

InVideo limits exports on its Plus plan (60 per month) but offers unlimited exports on the Max plan at $50/month. Fliki allocates annual minute pools (180 minutes on Standard, 600 on Premium). Colossyan follows the Synthesia model with per-minute allocations. VEED.io charges per editor seat with unlimited exports on paid plans.

Hidden Costs That Pricing Pages Won't Tell You

Every tool has costs that do not appear in the headline pricing. We found these by reading the fine print, testing edge cases, and tracking our actual bills during testing.

1. Overage Charges

The most painful surprise. Runway charges $0.50 per credit once you exceed your plan allocation, and a single 10-second Gen-3 video can consume 100 credits. Synthesia charges roughly $3 per extra minute. HeyGen's overage rate depends on your plan tier but can reach $4 per additional credit. Only VEED.io, InVideo (Max plan), and Descript avoid this problem by offering unlimited exports or generous allocations.

Watch out: Several tools auto-charge for overages without a confirmation prompt. Check your account settings to enable spending alerts or hard caps. Runway allows you to set a credit usage limit; Synthesia does not.

2. Watermark Removal

Free tiers on VEED.io, InVideo, Fliki, and Runway all include visible watermarks. Removing the watermark requires upgrading to a paid plan. In some cases the watermark is subtle (VEED.io places a small logo in the corner), while in others it is prominent and unmissable (InVideo overlays a large banner). If you need clean output for client work or public content, budget for at least the Starter tier on any tool.

3. Commercial Licensing

Not all plans include commercial usage rights. Kling's free tier output cannot be used commercially without upgrading. Runway restricts commercial use to paid plans. HeyGen and Synthesia include commercial rights on all paid tiers, but their free trials are explicitly non-commercial. Colossyan's Starter plan includes commercial rights, which is a notable advantage.

If you are creating videos for clients, ads, or any revenue-generating purpose, verify that your plan explicitly grants commercial use rights. Getting this wrong can lead to takedown notices or worse.

4. Premium Avatars and Voices

HeyGen's Instant Avatar cloning and custom avatar creation are available on the Creator plan and above, but some premium avatar styles are reserved for Business and Enterprise tiers. Synthesia locks its more realistic “Expressive Avatars” behind the Enterprise plan. Colossyan charges extra for custom avatar creation. Fliki's ultra-realistic voices require the Premium plan at $66/month.

5. API and Automation Access

If you plan to integrate AI video generation into your workflow via API, expect to pay a premium. HeyGen and Synthesia restrict API access to their Business and Enterprise plans respectively. Runway offers API access on all paid plans but charges separately for API usage at higher per-credit rates. Descript provides limited API functionality even on lower tiers.

6. Storage and Export Quality

VEED.io's free tier is capped at 720p and 10 minutes of storage. Descript's free plan limits cloud storage. Fliki's Standard plan exports at 1080p, but 4K requires Premium. InVideo's free plan caps exports at 720p with a 15-minute video duration limit. If high-resolution output matters, check the export specifications before committing.

What is the cheapest AI video generator in 2026?

Are AI video tool free plans actually usable?

It depends on the tool. Runway and Kling offer free credits for AI video generation, making them genuinely usable for experimentation. InVideo and VEED.io provide functional free editors with watermarks. However, most avatar-based tools like HeyGen and Synthesia offer only limited free trials rather than ongoing free plans. Free plans typically include watermarks, lower resolution, and restricted export options that limit professional use.

Is it cheaper to pay monthly or annually for AI video tools?

Annual billing saves you 15% to 40% compared to monthly billing across most AI video platforms. For example, HeyGen drops from $48/month to $29/month on an annual plan, saving you $228 per year. Descript and VEED.io offer 25–27% annual discounts. We recommend starting monthly to test the tool, then switching to annual once you have confirmed it fits your workflow.

What hidden costs should I watch for with AI video tools?

The most common hidden costs include overage charges when you exceed your monthly credit or minute allocation, watermark removal fees on lower tiers, extra charges for commercial licensing rights, premium avatar or voice add-ons, and API access fees for automation. Runway charges $0.50 per additional credit, and Synthesia charges roughly $3 per extra minute. Always check the fine print on commercial usage rights before committing to a plan.

Which AI video tool offers the best value for money?

For most users, Pictory offers the best overall value at $23/month with up to 30 videos per month and no watermarks.
