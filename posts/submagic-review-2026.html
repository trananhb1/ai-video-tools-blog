What Is Submagic?

Submagic is an AI-powered short-form video editing platform built specifically for creators who publish on TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts. You upload a video, and Submagic automatically generates animated captions, adds contextual B-roll, removes silences, corrects eye contact, and exports a platform-ready clip. The entire process takes less than two minutes for most videos.

Founded in May 2023 in Paris, France by David Zitoun and Tsi-fei Chan, Submagic has grown rapidly as a bootstrapped company with a 19-person team. The company reached $8 million in annual recurring revenue without external funding, which says something about product-market fit. Unlike general-purpose video editors, Submagic focuses narrowly on one workflow: taking raw short-form content and making it engaging enough to perform on social platforms.

The platform is entirely browser-based. There is nothing to install, no timeline editor to learn, and no rendering queue to manage locally. You upload, customize, and export. The simplicity is the product. Where tools like Descript offer a full editing suite and Opus Clip focuses on long-to-short repurposing, Submagic occupies a specific niche: making short-form video look polished with minimal effort.

Key Features: What Submagic Does Well

We tested Submagic over three weeks, running it through talking-head videos, podcast clips, tutorial recordings, and long-form repurposing workflows. Here is what each core feature delivers in practice.

Magic Captions

Submagic's flagship feature. Choose from 35+ animated caption styles including trending formats like Hormozi-style word-by-word highlights and MrBeast-style bold text. Captions are generated with 98.9% accuracy in 48+ languages. The AI auto-detects emphasis words, adds color highlights, and syncs animations to speech cadence. This is genuinely the best caption styling we have tested in any AI video tool.

Magic B-Roll

One-click contextual B-roll insertion powered by Storyblocks. Submagic analyzes your speech content and automatically inserts relevant stock footage clips at natural transition points. The B-roll is not always perfectly contextual for niche topics, but for common subjects like business, lifestyle, and tech, it saves significant editing time and breaks up talking-head monotony.

Magic Clips

Available as a $19/month add-on, Magic Clips uses AI to identify the most engaging segments of long-form videos and extract them as standalone short-form clips. Upload a 30-minute podcast or YouTube video and get multiple ready-to-post clips. Useful for repurposing, though Opus Clip remains stronger for dedicated long-to-short workflows with its virality scoring.

Silence Removal

Automatic detection and removal of dead air, pauses, and filler moments. One click and your video is tightened up without manual cutting. This alone saves hours per week for creators who record talking-head content. The detection is accurate and preserves natural speech rhythm without making the video feel unnaturally rushed.

AI Eye Contact Correction

Available on the Pro plan and above. Submagic uses AI to adjust your gaze so it appears you are looking directly at the camera, even when you were reading a script or looking at notes. The effect is subtle but noticeable. It works best on well-lit footage with a clear face. On lower quality or heavily angled shots, the correction can look slightly unnatural. Good lighting and a quality webcam make a real difference here — see our recommended gear for AI video creators.

Video Translation

Translate and dub your videos into 100+ languages with AI-generated voiceover. The translation quality is serviceable for social content and significantly faster than manual subtitle translation. Lip sync is approximate rather than precise, but for short-form content where viewers focus on captions and visuals, it gets the job done.

Auto-Emoji and Hashtags

Submagic automatically suggests relevant emojis to punctuate captions and generates optimized hashtags for each platform. The emoji placement adds visual variety to caption sequences, and the hashtag suggestions are based on content analysis rather than generic trending tags. A small feature, but it removes friction from the posting workflow.

Scheduled Publishing

Export and schedule posts directly to TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube from within Submagic. This eliminates the extra step of downloading, opening a scheduling tool, and re-uploading. For creators who batch-produce content, this closes the loop from editing to publishing in a single platform.

SRT/VTT Export

Beyond video export, Submagic lets you download caption files in SRT and VTT formats. This is valuable for creators who want to use Submagic's accurate transcription in other editing tools, or who need standalone subtitle files for accessibility compliance. The export preserves timing data accurately, so you can import the captions into Premiere Pro, DaVinci Resolve, or any other editor without retiming.

Platform-Ready Exports

Submagic exports in the correct aspect ratios and resolutions for each platform: 9:16 for TikTok, Reels, and Shorts; 1:1 for Instagram feed; 16:9 for YouTube. Resolution goes up to 1080p on all plans and 4K on the Business plan. The export process is fast for short clips but can be slower on longer videos, which is one of the few workflow friction points we encountered.

Submagic's free plan gives you 3 videos per month with a 90-second maximum duration, 1080p resolution, and basic animated captions. Videos include a Submagic watermark. No credit card is required to sign up.

This is enough to test the platform and see whether the caption styling and workflow suit your needs. You can get a genuine feel for the speed and quality of the AI processing. However, 3 videos per month with a watermark is not enough for any real production use. The 90-second limit also restricts you from testing longer content.

Compared to CapCut, which offers free auto captions with no video limit, Submagic's free tier is significantly more restrictive. The trade-off is that Submagic's caption styling is substantially more polished and varied. If you are evaluating the tool, the free plan is a useful test drive. If you are looking for a free ongoing solution, CapCut is the more practical choice.

Who Is Submagic For?

Submagic is designed for creators and businesses who produce short-form video content at volume and need to make it look polished without spending hours editing. The typical Submagic user is not looking for a full editing suite. They want to take raw footage and make it platform-ready as fast as possible. Common users include:

  • TikTok, Reels, and Shorts creators who publish daily or multiple times per week and need professional captions and effects without manual editing
  • YouTubers repurposing long-form content who want to extract and enhance clips from longer videos for social distribution
  • Social media managers and agencies handling multiple client accounts who need a fast, consistent workflow for short-form content production
  • Podcasters and interview creators who record talking-head content and need captions, B-roll, and silence removal to make clips engaging
  • Course creators and coaches who repurpose educational content into social clips for marketing
  • Solo entrepreneurs who create their own social content and need to maximize quality while minimizing time spent editing

If you are creating long-form video from scratch, need AI avatars, or want text-to-video generation, Submagic is not the right tool. It enhances existing video rather than generating new content. For those use cases, look at Synthesia for avatars, Fliki for text-to-video, or InVideo for template-based creation.

Final Verdict: Should You Use Submagic in 2026?

Submagic earns an 8.4 out of 10 rating from us. It does one thing exceptionally well: it takes raw short-form video and makes it look polished, professional, and platform-ready faster than any other tool we have tested. The animated caption styling is genuinely best-in-class. The silence removal, B-roll insertion, and eye contact correction features each add meaningful value. And the entire workflow takes under two minutes for most clips.

The platform's strength is also its limitation. Submagic is not a video editor. It does not give you timeline control, layer management, or the kind of granular customization that tools like Descript or even CapCut offer. If you need to do anything beyond Submagic's predefined enhancement workflow, you will need another tool. The B-roll matching can also miss the mark on niche or technical content, and the free plan is too restrictive for anything beyond a quick test.

The pricing is fair for what you get, but it is worth noting that CapCut offers free auto captions, which makes Submagic harder to justify on a pure cost basis. What you are paying for is the quality gap: Submagic's caption styles look significantly better, the accuracy is higher, and the additional features like silence removal and eye contact correction have no free equivalent at this quality level.

For TikTok creators, Reels producers, YouTube Shorts publishers, social media managers, and anyone who needs to turn raw talking-head footage into engaging short-form content at volume, Submagic is the best dedicated tool available. It will not replace your full editing workflow, but it will make the caption and enhancement step faster and better than anything else on the market right now.
