What Is Movavi Video Editor?

Movavi Video Editor is a desktop video editing application for Windows and macOS developed by Movavi, a software company founded in 2004. Unlike the wave of cloud-based AI video generators that have dominated the market in recent years, Movavi takes a different approach: it is a traditional timeline-based editor enhanced with AI-powered features that genuinely improve the editing workflow without requiring you to learn a new paradigm.

The editor is built for people who want professional-looking results without spending weeks learning keyboard shortcuts. The interface uses a familiar drag-and-drop timeline with a media library, preview window, and properties panel. If you have ever used iMovie, you will feel at home immediately. If you are coming from no editing experience at all, Movavi includes step-by-step tutorials built into the interface itself.

What has changed in 2026 is the addition of genuinely useful AI features. Movavi now includes AI background removal without a green screen, AI noise reduction for audio and video, motion tracking, AI upscaling for low-resolution footage, and automatic color correction. These are not gimmicks — they are the features that used to require expensive plugins or professional-grade software like DaVinci Resolve, now accessible in a $15.95/month editor. AI-Powered Features: What Sets Movavi Apart in 2026

Movavi has steadily added AI features over the past two years, and the 2026 edition delivers a set that genuinely competes with editors two or three times its price. Here is what the AI can do.

AI Background Removal

Remove or replace video backgrounds without a green screen. The AI detects the subject and separates it from the background in real time. It works best with well-lit footage and clear subject-background contrast, but even in moderate conditions the results are usable for YouTube and social content. This feature alone used to require dedicated software or After Effects plugins.

AI Noise Removal

Reduce background noise from audio tracks with one click. The AI identifies and suppresses hum, hiss, wind noise, and ambient sound while preserving voice clarity. Particularly useful for creators recording in untreated rooms or outdoor environments. The results rival dedicated audio tools like Audacity noise reduction but without the manual configuration.

Motion Tracking

Attach text, stickers, or effects to moving objects in your footage. The tracking algorithm follows the selected object across frames, keeping overlays pinned to the right position. Useful for highlighting products in review videos, adding name tags to people in vlogs, or creating dynamic text that follows the action.

AI Upscaling

Enhance the resolution of low-quality footage using AI. Particularly useful for repurposing older content, improving phone footage shot in lower resolutions, or rescuing clips from webcams that did not record at full HD. The upscaling adds genuine detail rather than simple sharpening, though it works better on 720p-to-1080p than on extremely low-resolution sources.

Auto Color Correction

Automatically adjust white balance, exposure, contrast, and saturation to produce natural-looking footage. The AI analyzes each clip and applies scene-appropriate corrections. It is not a replacement for manual color grading on professional projects, but for quick corrections on YouTube videos and social content, it saves significant time.

Chroma Key (Green Screen)

For creators with a physical green screen, Movavi includes a full chroma key tool with tolerance and edge-smoothing controls. Combined with the AI background removal, you have two options for background replacement: AI-only for quick work, or chroma key for pixel-perfect results with controlled lighting.

How Good Are the AI Features Really?

Honest assessment: Movavi's AI features are genuinely useful for their target audience but they are not cutting-edge. The AI background removal works well in 70-80% of scenarios — well-lit rooms with clear subject separation produce clean results. Complex backgrounds, moving subjects with flowing hair, and low-light conditions still trip it up. If you need flawless background removal every time, a physical green screen with Movavi's chroma key is still the better path.

The AI noise removal is the standout. It handles common recording issues — air conditioning hum, keyboard clicks, room echo — with minimal effort and minimal loss of voice quality. For creators who cannot afford acoustic treatment or dedicated microphones, this feature alone can be worth the subscription.

Motion tracking is solid but not After Effects-level. It tracks well on high-contrast objects but can lose lock on fast-moving or small subjects. For the use cases Movavi targets — attaching labels, adding callouts, basic object tracking — it works well enough.

Core Editing Features

Beyond the AI additions, Movavi Video Editor delivers a complete set of editing tools that cover everything most creators need. Here is what you get in the timeline editor.

  • Multi-track timeline editing — Layer video, audio, titles, and effects on multiple tracks. Drag to trim, split, and rearrange clips with snap-to-grid precision.
  • 180+ transitions — Cross dissolves, wipes, 3D transitions, and artistic effects. Each is adjustable for duration and style.
  • Titles and text overlays — Pre-designed title templates for intros, lower thirds, credits, and captions. Full font, color, and animation control.
  • Picture-in-picture — Overlay one video on another with adjustable size and position. Essential for reaction videos, tutorials, and gaming content.
  • Slow motion and time-lapse — Speed up or slow down footage with frame interpolation for smoother slow-motion playback.
  • Keyframe animation — Animate position, scale, rotation, and opacity over time. More limited than After Effects but sufficient for zoom effects, pan animations, and basic motion graphics.
  • Audio editing — Built-in audio equalizer, beat detection for syncing cuts to music, volume keyframing, and audio ducking to automatically lower music when speech is detected.
  • Video stabilization — Reduce shake from handheld footage. Works well on mild to moderate shake but struggles with extreme movement.
  • Export presets — One-click export for YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, and other platforms with optimized resolution and bitrate settings. Also supports custom export with full codec control.
  • LUT support — Import and apply custom color lookup tables for consistent color grading across projects.

The editing experience is smooth and responsive. Movavi handles 1080p footage without issues on modest hardware, and 4K editing is supported on machines with 8GB+ RAM and a dedicated GPU. Rendering times are competitive — in our testing, a 10-minute 1080p video with transitions and effects exported in approximately 4 minutes on a mid-range system with hardware acceleration enabled.

For most video creators, either the standalone Video Editor or the Video Suite is the right choice. If you create screen recordings or tutorials, the Suite saves money versus buying the editor and recorder separately.

The Desktop-Only Trade-Off

Movavi being a desktop-only application is both its biggest limitation and its greatest advantage. On the limitation side, you cannot collaborate with team members in real time, access your projects from multiple devices, or edit on the go from a tablet. Cloud-based editors like Descript solve these problems elegantly.

On the advantage side, desktop editing means your footage never leaves your computer. Rendering happens locally using your hardware, so export times are predictable and not dependent on server queues. You own your project files. You can work without an internet connection. And for creators who work with sensitive footage — client projects, unreleased content, personal videos — keeping everything local is a legitimate privacy benefit.
