Which HeyGen plan is best for a small business or agency?

Business at $124/mo on annual billing ($149 monthly) plus $20/additional seat is the right tier the moment you have a second person making videos — and it's also the moment SCORM, integrations, and 4K output show up. As of April 2026, Business includes:

  • 1,000 credits in a shared team pool (about 50 minutes of premium Avatar V across all seats)
  • 60-minute maximum video duration (up from 30 min on Creator/Pro)
  • 4K output for broadcast and large-screen use
  • SCORM export for LMS integration — new on Business in 2026, was Enterprise-only previously
  • Native integrations: Zapier, HubSpot, Make, n8n — lets you trigger video generation from CRM events
  • Brand kit for consistent visual identity
  • 5 custom digital twins across the team
  • API access bundled in (Pro and Creator do not include API)

The watch-out: Business has fewer credits (1,000) than Pro (2,000) despite costing more. You're paying for team features, 4K output, integrations, brand kit, and SCORM — not raw credit volume. A solo user who needs maximum credits should stay on Pro at $82.50/mo annual; a small team of 3-5 should pay the Business premium for the collaboration layer.

Math example: a 4-person agency at Business runs $124 + ($20 × 3) = $184/mo on annual billing for the whole team plus 1,000 shared credits. The same 4 people on individual Creator plans = $96/mo, but with no shared workspace, no SCORM, no integrations, and four separate billing relationships.

How do I actually use the HeyGen free tier without hitting walls?

The free plan as of April 2026 gives you:

  • 3 videos per month, each capped at 1 minute (down from 3 min in 2025)
  • 720p with a HeyGen watermark on every export
  • 500+ stock avatars and 1 custom digital twin — you can train your own face on the free plan, which is the underrated part most reviewers miss
  • 30+ trial languages (the full 175+ unlocks at Creator)

How to use the free tier productively: spend your 3 videos this month testing things you can't test in a screenshot — train one custom digital twin from a 15-second selfie video, render it speaking your own script in your worst-case language (e.g. for a US creator, render Tagalog or Vietnamese to see if lip-sync holds), and check whether Avatar V's eye contact reads natural to you. Three videos is enough to make a confident upgrade decision. It is not enough to ship anything externally because of the watermark and 1-minute cap.

If you want a free tier you can actually ship from, Vidnoz at 3 minutes per day or CapCut (no avatar) are the better picks. Then upgrade to HeyGen Creator when realism becomes the bottleneck.

How HeyGen's credit system works (and where it bites)

HeyGen meters paid plans with credits, not flat per-video pricing. Every render that uses a premium model consumes credits from your monthly pool. The pool resets at the start of each billing cycle and unused credits are gone.

Action Credit cost Notes
Avatar V or IV video ~20 credits/min Premium realism, 0.840 face similarity (Avatar V)
Standard avatar video ~1 credit/min Good quality, less natural than Avatar V
Video translation Varies by length Full lip-sync dub, scales with input length
Interactive Avatar (streaming) Per-session Real-time, highest consumption rate
Talking Photo ~1 credit/min Animate a static photo
Premium Credit Pack $15 = 300 credits Top-up for monthly subscribers; $150/yr for annual

Key rules to know:

  • Credits do not roll over. A quiet month is wasted budget. Schedule batch render days at month-end if you're underusing.
  • You can buy Premium Credit Packs mid-month at $15 per 300 credits ($0.05/credit) — that's $1/min of additional Avatar V video.
  • Avatar V/IV and video translation are the biggest credit consumers. Mixing in standard avatars for low-stakes renders stretches budget significantly.
  • Standard avatars at ~1 credit/min mean Creator's 200 credits = 200 minutes of standard avatar video. The credit math only feels tight if you only ever use Avatar V.

Hidden costs and what's NOT included

1. Premium Credit Pack overages

If you exhaust your monthly credits, Premium Credit Packs are $15 for 300 credits (monthly subs) or $150/yr for annual subs. That's $0.05/credit, or $1/min for additional Avatar V video. Not steep, but it adds up if you underestimated your video volume.

2. API pricing is separate from web plan

The HeyGen API uses its own pay-as-you-go credit pool, billed independently from your web plan. Even on Business (which bundles API access), heavy API consumption can run separate from your web credits. Budget for API costs on top of subscription.

3. Custom avatar creation needs source footage

Business and Enterprise include 5+ custom digital twins, but you have to record the source footage. If you hire a videographer for that capture session, that cost isn't covered. The free plan now lets you train 1 digital twin without hiring anyone — useful as a sanity check before committing.

4. No built-in timeline editor

HeyGen renders avatar video; it doesn't ship a timeline editor with transitions, motion graphics, or multi-track audio. Most users pair HeyGen with Descript ($24/mo) or CapCut for finishing. Factor it into your stack cost.

5. Annual lock-in risk

Annual saves 17% across the board, but you pay upfront and cancellation after the first 14 days is non-refundable. If you're new to HeyGen, start monthly for two cycles, then switch.

6. Video translation eats credits faster than expected

Translating a 5-minute video into one additional language can consume a meaningful chunk of your monthly credits. If you plan to localize into 5+ languages monthly, budget for Pro from the start.
