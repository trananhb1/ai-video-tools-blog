Platform Strategy: TikTok

TikTok at a Glance

15–60 seconds 9:16 vertical Auto-captions essential Hook in first 1–2 seconds Trending audio matters

TikTok remains the fastest-growing platform for organic reach. Its algorithm rewards content quality over follower count, making it the best platform for brands to go viral without a massive existing audience.

Best AI Tool for TikTok: Opus Clip

Opus Clip is the single most effective AI tool for TikTok content. Its core function — extracting the most engaging clips from longer videos and reformatting them for vertical platforms — maps perfectly to the TikTok content model.

How to use Opus Clip for TikTok:

  1. Feed it source content. Upload a webinar, podcast episode, YouTube video, or any long-form recording. Opus Clip accepts videos up to 3 hours.
  2. Let the AI extract clips. The algorithm identifies hooks, emotional peaks, complete thought segments, and viral-potential moments, producing 10–20 clips per video.
  3. Auto-format for TikTok. Each clip is reframed to 9:16 vertical with AI face tracking, animated word-by-word captions, and a virality score.
  4. Cherry-pick the best 3–5 clips. Review the virality scores, tweak captions if needed, and export directly.

TikTok-Specific Tips

  • The 15–60 second sweet spot. TikTok’s algorithm favors videos that are watched to completion. Keep clips tight — 30–45 seconds is the ideal range for most marketing content.
  • Captions are non-negotiable. Over 80% of TikTok users watch with sound off at least some of the time. Burned-in animated captions are essential, and Opus Clip handles this automatically.
  • Ride trending formats. TikTok rewards content that uses trending sounds, transitions, and formats. Watch the Discover page daily and adapt your AI-generated clips to current trends.
  • Post 1–3 times daily. Volume matters on TikTok more than any other platform. AI makes this sustainable.

Platform Strategy: YouTube Shorts

YouTube Shorts at a Glance

30–60 seconds 9:16 vertical Hook in first 2 seconds Loops increase watch time Drives long-form subscribers

YouTube Shorts now surpass 70 billion daily views. For marketers, the unique advantage of Shorts is the bridge to long-form content — a viral Short can drive thousands of subscribers to your main channel and long-form videos where deeper conversion happens.

Best AI Tools for YouTube Shorts: Opus Clip + InVideo

Opus Clip handles repurposing existing long-form content into Shorts (the same workflow described in the TikTok section). For creating original Shorts from scratch — where you start with an idea rather than an existing video — InVideo is the strongest option.

InVideo for original Shorts:

  1. Describe your video idea in plain text (e.g., “5 time management tips for entrepreneurs, energetic tone, 45 seconds”).
  2. InVideo generates a complete video with stock footage, text overlays, transitions, and background music.
  3. Edit any element — swap clips, adjust text, change the voiceover — in the browser-based editor.
  4. Export in 9:16 vertical format, ready for YouTube Shorts.

YouTube Shorts Tips

  • The first 2 seconds decide everything. YouTube’s algorithm measures immediate retention. If viewers swipe away in the first 2 seconds, the algorithm stops promoting the Short. Open with a question, a bold claim, or a visual surprise.
  • 30–60 seconds performs best. Unlike TikTok where 15-second clips can work, Shorts that run 30–60 seconds tend to get more impressions because the algorithm rewards total watch time.
  • Create a loop. End the Short in a way that connects back to the beginning, encouraging viewers to watch it again. Repeated views dramatically boost algorithmic ranking.
  • Add a subscribe CTA. Shorts are subscriber acquisition machines. Include a brief “subscribe for more” prompt, either verbally or as a text overlay in the final seconds.

Step-by-Step Workflow: From Content Idea to Published Video

Here is the exact workflow a social media marketer can follow to go from a content idea to published videos across four platforms in under two hours.

Step 1: Content Idea and Script (15 minutes)

Start with a single content idea. Use ChatGPT or Claude to generate a script tailored to your primary platform. Specify the target length, tone, and a strong opening hook. For example:

  • Input: “Write a 60-second TikTok script about 3 ways small businesses waste money on ads, casual and direct tone, open with a bold hook.”
  • Output: A ready-to-use script with hook, three talking points, and a CTA.

Step 2: Create the Primary Video (20 minutes)

Choose the AI tool that matches your content type:

  • Talking head / thought leadership: Paste the script into Synthesia or HeyGen. Select your avatar and generate.
  • Ad-style / product video: Paste the script into Zebracat. It generates footage, overlays, and voiceover automatically.
  • Voiceover + stock footage: Use Fliki to generate the voiceover and pair it with footage in InVideo.

Step 3: Platform Optimization (15 minutes)

Adapt the primary video for each platform:

  • TikTok: Trim to 30–60 seconds, add animated captions, ensure 9:16 format.
  • Instagram Reels: Same format but optimize the cover frame and add 3–5 relevant hashtags.
  • LinkedIn: Re-record or adjust the script for a more professional tone. Add burned-in subtitles. Consider square (1:1) format for feed visibility.
  • YouTube Shorts: Ensure the hook lands in the first 2 seconds. Add a subscribe CTA at the end.

Step 4: Repurpose Long-Form Content (10 minutes)

If you have existing long-form content (webinars, podcasts, YouTube videos), feed it into Opus Clip to extract additional short-form clips. One 30-minute video typically yields 10–15 usable clips — enough for 3–4 days of multi-platform posting.

Step 5: Schedule and Publish (10 minutes)

Upload all videos to your scheduling tool (Buffer, Later, or Hootsuite). Stagger posting times for each platform based on your audience analytics. A good starting schedule:

  • TikTok: 11am and 7pm local time
  • Instagram Reels: 12pm and 6pm local time
  • LinkedIn: Tuesday–Thursday, 8–10am local time
  • YouTube Shorts: 2–4pm local time
Tip: Batch your content creation. Set aside one morning per week to create all your videos for the next 7 days. AI tools make batching incredibly efficient — you can produce an entire week of multi-platform content in a single 3–4 hour session.

Pro Tips for AI Social Media Video

After testing dozens of AI video tools across hundreds of social media posts, here are the strategies that consistently produce the best results.

1. A/B Test Your Hooks

The first 1–3 seconds of any social media video determine whether someone watches or scrolls past. Use AI to generate 3–5 different hooks for the same content, post them on different days, and let the data tell you which style resonates with your audience. Common hook patterns that perform well:

  • Bold claim: “Stop wasting money on Facebook ads. Here is what works instead.”
  • Question: “Why do 90% of small businesses fail at social media?”
  • Controversy: “Nobody needs a social media manager anymore.”
  • Result reveal: “We grew from 0 to 50K followers in 90 days using only AI tools.”

2. Batch Create Content Weekly

AI tools are most efficient when used in batches. Instead of creating one video at a time, set aside a dedicated content production block each week:

  1. Monday morning: Write 5–7 scripts with ChatGPT (30 minutes).
  2. Monday afternoon: Generate all videos with your AI tool stack (1–2 hours).
  3. Tuesday: Review, make minor edits, and schedule everything for the week (30 minutes).

This approach turns video production into a predictable system rather than a daily scramble.

3. Repurpose One Long Video Into 10+ Shorts

The highest-leverage AI video strategy is creating one substantial piece of content and using Opus Clip to extract multiple short clips. A single 20-minute webinar or YouTube video yields:

  • 10–15 short clips (30–60 seconds each)
  • 3–5 platform-specific versions of each clip (TikTok, Reels, Shorts, LinkedIn)
  • 2+ weeks of daily posting across all four platforms

This means one recording session can generate 40–60 individual social media posts. No other content strategy delivers that level of output from a single input.

Pro Tip: Since one recording session feeds weeks of content, investing in a quality microphone and ring light pays for itself many times over. See our recommended gear for AI video creators for affordable setups that make every clip look and sound professional.

4. Match the Native Style of Each Platform

The biggest mistake marketers make is posting the same video on every platform. Each platform has a distinct culture and visual language:

  • TikTok: Raw, fast-paced, personality-driven. Imperfection is accepted. Trending sounds and formats matter.
  • Instagram Reels: Slightly more polished. Aesthetic matters. Cover frames should look good on your grid.
  • LinkedIn: Professional, insight-driven, calm pacing. Subtitles mandatory. No trending sounds.
  • YouTube Shorts: Educational or entertaining. Strong hooks. Longer than TikTok (30–60 seconds vs 15–30).

5. Use AI Voiceover Strategically

AI voiceover from Fliki or Murf AI works well for explainer content, product showcases, and listicle-style videos. It is less effective for personal brand content where your audience expects your real voice. Know when to use AI voice and when to record yourself — the best marketers blend both.

6. Track and Iterate

AI tools give you the speed to test aggressively. Post 4–5 variations of the same content with different hooks, lengths, and formats. After two weeks, analyze which patterns perform best on each platform and double down on what works. The speed of AI iteration means you can optimize your content strategy in weeks rather than months.

Can AI-generated videos perform well on TikTok and Instagram?

Yes, AI-generated videos regularly achieve strong performance on TikTok and Instagram. The key is matching the native style of each platform. How do I repurpose one video for multiple social media platforms?

Start with a single long-form video (5–30 minutes) as your source content. Use Opus Clip to extract 10–15 short clips automatically. Do I need to disclose that a video was made with AI?

Platform policies vary but are converging toward transparency. TikTok and Meta (Instagram) now require labeling AI-generated content that could be mistaken for real footage, particularly deepfakes and synthetic talking heads. AI-edited content (caption generation, clip extraction, background removal) generally does not require disclosure. When using AI avatars from tools like Synthesia or HeyGen, adding a brief disclosure is both ethically sound and increasingly required by platform terms of service.
