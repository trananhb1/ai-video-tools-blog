The 5-step Sora export (works on desktop and mobile)

Every video you generated on Sora is stored on OpenAI's servers and gets wiped on April 26. The export tool packages them into a single ZIP with the prompts that created them. The whole process takes about 5 minutes of your time plus 1–24 hours of waiting for OpenAI to build the archive. Start now.

  1. Step 1: Go to sora.chatgpt.com/exports/me. Open sora.chatgpt.com/exports/me in any browser. Sign in with the same OpenAI account you used to generate your videos. ChatGPT Plus, Pro, and Team accounts all have export access. If you used multiple OpenAI accounts to make Sora videos, you'll need to repeat the export on each one.
  2. Step 2: Click "Request my data export". One button, one click. OpenAI queues the job server-side. You won't see a progress bar — the page just confirms the request is in.
  3. Step 3: Wait for the email. OpenAI emails the address tied to your account when the archive is ready. As of April 22, 2026, typical turnaround is 1–4 hours. Users with hundreds of generations have reported waits up to 24 hours. Check your spam folder if nothing arrives in 6 hours — the sender is noreply@tm.openai.com.
  4. Step 4: Download the ZIP archive. The email contains a single download link valid for 7 days. The ZIP includes every video you generated (MP4 files), the text prompt that produced each one, generation timestamps, and a metadata.json file. Big libraries can run several gigabytes — download on Wi-Fi, not cellular.
  5. Step 5: Verify your files. Open the ZIP and check the video count matches what you see in the Sora interface. Play 2–3 random clips to confirm they're not corrupted. If anything is missing or broken, request a second export immediately — the tool only works while the system is still online.

3 warnings that catch people out

Most export problems come from waiting too long or assuming the export covers more than it does. As of April 22, 2026, here are the three issues we're seeing creators run into:

The API stays online until September 24, 2026, so you have more time — but no new API keys are being issued.
  • Bulk export only works through the data export tool. You can right-click and save individual videos from the Sora interface, but there's no built-in "download all" button. The data export is the only way to grab the entire library at once. If you only want a handful of clips, manual download works fine.

Why is Sora shutting down?

OpenAI announced the two-stage Sora shutdown on March 24, 2026. The reasons given:

  • Compute costs. Video generation consumed GPU capacity OpenAI needs for its core language model products.
  • Declining users. Active users fell from a peak of around 1 million to under 500,000, per TechCrunch.
  • Copyright pressure. Ongoing litigation over training data added legal risk OpenAI chose not to carry.
  • Enterprise pivot. Resources are being redirected to coding agents and enterprise products ahead of OpenAI's planned IPO.

Typical export turnaround as of April 22, 2026 is 1–4 hours. Heavy users with hundreds of generations have reported up to 24 hours. OpenAI is processing a backlog as the deadline approaches, so request the export now rather than the day before.

Will I lose my Sora videos if I don't export?

Yes. Any Sora video that exists only on OpenAI's servers is permanently deleted on April 26, 2026. Videos already downloaded to your computer or saved to other platforms remain yours under your existing license. Request the data export at sora.chatgpt.com/exports/me before the deadline.
