Choose an avatar that matches your brand's tone. For premium products, select a professional, well-dressed avatar. For casual or youth-oriented products, pick a more relaxed presenter. 7 Common Mistakes to Avoid with AI E-Commerce Videos

  1. Making videos too long. Product demo videos should be 60-90 seconds maximum. Social ads should be 15-30 seconds. Attention spans are short; get to the value proposition within the first 5 seconds.
  2. Not including a clear call-to-action. Every video needs to tell the viewer exactly what to do next: "Add to Cart," "Shop Now," "Use code X for 20% off." Without a CTA, you generate interest but not conversions.
  3. Using the wrong avatar tone. A corporate avatar presenting a fun, casual product (or vice versa) creates a disconnect. Match the avatar's energy and style to your brand and target audience.
  4. Ignoring mobile formatting. Over 70% of e-commerce traffic is mobile. Create vertical (9:16) versions of your videos for social, and ensure your product page videos render properly on mobile screens.
  5. Creating one video and stopping. The power of AI tools is volume and iteration. Create multiple variations, A/B test different hooks, and produce videos for every product — not just your bestsellers.
  6. Skipping captions and subtitles. 85% of social media video is watched without sound. Always add captions. Most AI tools generate them automatically; make sure they are enabled.
  7. Not localizing for international markets. If you sell globally, translate your top-performing videos into your customers' languages. With AI tools supporting 40-140 languages, there is no reason not to.
Common Pitfall: Do not use AI-generated videos to misrepresent your product. Ensure the product shown in your video matches what customers will receive. AI makes production easy; use that efficiency to create honest, helpful content that builds trust and reduces returns.
