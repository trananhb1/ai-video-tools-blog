1. HeyGen — the benchmark you're trying to beat

H

HeyGen

$24/mo annual · Avatar V · 175+ languages

HeyGen is the platform 91% of readers who click through this site actually end up buying. Worth stating up front because it frames what the alternatives need to beat.

What HeyGen gives you at $24/mo (Creator, annual billing): unlimited video output, Avatar IV and Avatar V, 175+ languages with lip-sync, voice cloning, 300+ templates, and a free tier with 3 videos per month at 3 minutes each. Who keeps HeyGen: solo creators, sales teams doing personalised video at scale, marketers producing ads and social content, global brands translating existing footage. If your videos are under 5 minutes and viewer-facing, nothing on this list beats it.

2. Synthesia — the enterprise training pick

S

Synthesia

$18/mo annual · 230+ avatars · SCORM + SSO

Synthesia is the only tool on this list that ships SOC 2 Type II, SAML SSO, and SCORM export together. That's why Fortune 500 L&D teams default here even though HeyGen's avatars look more lifelike.

What Synthesia gives you at $18/mo (Starter, annual billing): 10 minutes of video per month, 125+ avatars, 3 personal avatars, logo removal, video download, 140+ languages, no watermark. The Creator tier at $64/mo annual adds API access, multi-avatar scenes, and interactive video with branching and quizzes. Enterprise (custom pricing, typically low five figures for small teams) adds SCORM export, SSO, unlimited minutes, and 240+ avatars.

Where Synthesia loses: the 10-minute monthly cap on Starter is brutal if you produce regularly (roughly one 2-minute video per week). Video translation stays locked behind Enterprise. Avatars don't move with HeyGen Avatar V's micro-expression subtlety — they look like well-trained corporate presenters, which is perfect for training and wrong for social ads.

Who picks Synthesia over HeyGen: HR onboarding teams, compliance/regulatory training programs, university L&D, anyone whose buyer says "needs SCORM" or "needs SSO". Final Verdict: HeyGen still wins for most 2026 buyers

After pricing nine tools head-to-head, the honest answer is that HeyGen remains the right default for most people searching for HeyGen alternatives. Avatar V's 0.840 face similarity is a real moat, the $24/mo Creator plan is unlimited, and no alternative on this list beats it on both realism and price together.

Where the alternatives win:

  • Synthesia wins if SCORM, SOC 2, or SSO is in the buyer's requirements doc. That's not negotiable — HeyGen doesn't ship those, Synthesia does.
  • Vidnoz wins if budget is <$20/month or you're testing volume. $14.99/mo and a genuinely useful free tier is real.
  • Pictory wins if you're turning articles into video without an on-camera presenter.
  • Arcads and MakeUGC win if you're a DTC brand producing UGC-style ads. MakeUGC ($39/mo annual) is the budget entry; Arcads ($110/mo) is the scale play.
  • Fliki wins alongside Pictory for text-to-video workflows — different job from avatars, but ~$20/mo annual and genuinely useful.
  • Colossyan and DeepBrain are niche picks — branching scenarios and real-time avatars respectively. If those aren't your hard requirements, skip them.

In hands-on testing across all six, Avatar V is the only engine where first-time viewers don't immediately flag the output as AI-generated. That's the spec that matters most for viewer-facing video, and it's why HeyGen converts 91% of tracked affiliate clicks on this site. If your use case is solo, marketing, sales, or global content — start free on HeyGen and only move sideways if a specific feature you need isn't there.
